Global health and poverty is often treated as one of the most accessible entry points into effective altruism. Some interventions are unusually measurable, and comparatively modest additional resources can still have substantial effects.

Useful starting questions include:

- Which health or income problems create the largest burden?
- Which interventions have the strongest evidence?
- Where could an additional career move, donation, or research contribution make the biggest difference?
