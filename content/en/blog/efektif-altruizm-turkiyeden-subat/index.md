## **Announcements**

1. **Effective Altruism Turkey University Students Retreat**

We brought together students interested in effective altruism from various universities in Turkey and organized workshops on topics such as impactful careers, rationality, and community building. Nineteen participants who filled out the feedback form rated the retreat 8.9 out of 10 on average for meeting their expectations.

*Thank you to all participants. We hope to see you again at our next event.✨*
2. **[Effective Animal Advocacy Reading Group](https://docs.google.com/forms/d/e/1FAIpQLSeQ8oV_5e259uZS9wEWvOFAObcpePXh96Da4GV4rshq5Z-UrQ/viewform?usp=dialog)**

What can we do to increase the strength of the animal advocacy movement? Is it possible to develop more effective strategies, critically evaluate existing approaches and make a real difference for animals? These questions form the basis of the Effective Animal Advocacy Reading Group. This 8-week online reading group offers a learning and discussion platform for anyone who wants to create a better world for animals. With this program, participants will have comprehensive access to important concepts and discussions in the field, starting from the basic level. You will have the opportunity to explore different perspectives and develop your own activism practice in a friendly and supportive environment.

**Application deadline: March 4**
3. **[EA Ankara's Introduction to Effective Altruism Program](https://docs.google.com/forms/d/e/1FAIpQLSdl-nBBE1nvLuTSRr98X1N1M96j3evds-QAOpMIlydimsnCIg/viewform?usp=dialog)**

A six-week discussion group designed to identify the world’s priority problems, develop the most effective solutions, and encourage individuals to take responsibility for those solutions. In this face-to-face program run by EA Ankara, you can discuss how we can better help people and other sentient beings on both a theoretical and practical level, explore different perspectives, and develop your own path to impact in a friendly and supportive environment. The first meeting will take place on Saturday, March 1 at 16:00 at Kızılay Selanik Route Cafe, with a presentation by Fırat Akova, and will be open to everyone interested.

**Application deadline: March 4**
4. **[Future Impact Group](https://futureimpact.group/)**

The Oxford-based research group offers a 12-week, part-time research program for researchers working in the fields of AI security, policy and philosophy. In this program, where various topics focusing on solving the world's most urgent problems are selected, you can apply to the field of your choice and create a global impact together with mentors who are experts in their fields.

**Application deadline: March 8**

[AIM Research Program](https://www.aimresearchprogram.com/) Applications for 2025 are open. This 12-week, online and free program aims to equip participants with the skills to analyse, compare and develop strategies for effective charities and interventions.

This five-part online training series, organized in collaboration with Mercy For Animals, New Roots Institute, and The Humane League, is designed for new activists who want to learn and practice the basic skills of advocating within the community while avoiding infighting. ## **Selected Readings**

[Teaching AI to reason: this year's most important story](https://benjamintodd.substack.com/p/teaching-ai-to-reason-this-years)

In 2024, artificial intelligence made a groundbreaking improvement in its reasoning ability thanks to reinforcement learning. Benjamin Todd states that new AI models can answer scientific questions at the PhD level, surpassing human experts in coding and mathematics, and predicts that we can reach superhuman artificial intelligence capacity in science and coding within two years. He emphasizes that new developments could enable the beginning of a cycle of self-improvement of artificial intelligence, and that this is "the most important (scientific, economic and social) development in the world at the moment."

[We need a new Artesunate - the miracle drug fades — EA Forum](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/RLCfqw9DKchfngv3f/we-need-a-new-artesunate-the-miracle-drug-fades)

Nick Laing, founder of OneDay Health, draws attention to the need for new drugs due to increasing resistance to Artesunate, known as the "miracle drug" in the treatment of malaria. Although this drug, which revolutionized malaria treatment, has saved the lives of more than a million people in the last decade, its effectiveness is gradually decreasing. Laing points out the importance and urgency of allocating more resources to the search for new vaccines and treatments to eliminate malaria, along with climate change, increasing resistance to medicated bed nets (bed nets used to prevent malaria transmission) and decreasing international funds.
