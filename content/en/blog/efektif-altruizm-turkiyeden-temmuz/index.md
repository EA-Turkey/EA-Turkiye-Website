In this issue, we bring you EA Koç University's events, books you can access for free, various courses and reading groups, global conferences you can attend, and more.

#### **🏫🙋🏻Meet EA Koç!**

EA Koç University, which has introduced many people to Effective Altruism through the events it has organized since its founding in the 2023 fall semester, is an active university community in Turkey. To learn more about its events or get in touch, you can visit its **[Instagram account](https://www.instagram.com/eakocuni/)**.

The most recent event organized by EA Koç University is 'Peter Singer's Greatest Goodness Book Reading Group'. In this event, participants are divided into groups and a discussion environment is provided where individuals are listened to with respect and tolerance.

#### **👩🏼‍💻[Effective Altruism Virtual Programs - In-Depth EA Program](https://www.effectivealtruism.org/virtual-programs/in-depth-program)**

## OUR RECOMMENDED CONTENT 📑🎧

What exactly is Effective Altruism? What exactly does being an “Effective Altruist” mean? In this issue, we wanted to include two articles that give opposite answers to these questions. In the first article, Helen Toner argues that effective altruism should actually be about the question: “How can I do the most good using the resources I have?” rather than a set of principles or ideas.

James Fodor, on the other hand, argues that Effective Altruism is much more than a question, it corresponds to an ideology or belief system. Finally, let us leave you with the articles by stating that James Fodor did not say this from a critical point of view!

**❓[Effective Altruism is a Question (not an ideology)](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/FpjQMYQmS3rWewZ83/effective-altruism-is-a-question-not-an-ideology)**

**💡[Effective Altruism is an ideology, not a question](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/uxFvTnzSgw8uakNBp/effective-altruism-is-an-ideology-not-just-a-question)**
